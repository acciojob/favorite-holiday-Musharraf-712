Kashmir

Kashmir is the northernmost geographical region of the Indian subcontinent. Until the mid-19th century, the term Kashmir denoted only the Kashmir Valley between the Great Himalayas and the Pir Panjal Range.

  1. Kashmir is a beautiful region located in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent.
  2. The region is known for its breathtaking landscapes, including Dal Lake and Gulmarg.
  3. Kashmir has a rich cultural heritage influenced by Hindu, Muslim, and Buddhist traditions.
  4. Tourism, handicrafts, and agriculture are the main sources of its economy.
  • Rogan Josh – a flavorful lamb curry cooked with aromatic Kashmiri spices.
  • Yakhni – a yogurt-based mutton curry seasoned with mild spices and fennel.
  • Modur Pulao – sweet saffron rice cooked with milk, nuts, and ghee.
  • Gushtaba – minced mutton balls cooked in a rich, creamy yogurt gravy.
  • Kahwa – a traditional Kashmiri green tea infused with saffron, almonds, and spices.
